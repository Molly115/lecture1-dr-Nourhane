LECTURE ONE

Evaluation Mindset

Transitioning from a casual user to a professional digital evaluator. Learn to see what others only use.

01. The Psychological Hook

5 MINS

Harnessing Frustration

Before diving into theory, we must acknowledge the "Emotional Gap." Every student has experienced digital anger. By asking about the last app that made them angry, we identify the exact moment Marketing fails.

"When you hit a broken button, you don't just feel annoyed; you lose trust in the entire brand. That loss of trust costs millions."

02. Defining the Interface

10 MINS

The "Device" is Not Hardware

Students often confuse the device (Smartphone) with the experience (Interface). We redefine the Digital Device as any digital touchpoint where a user makes a decision.

  • Websites: The digital storefront.
  • Apps: The functional tool.
  • Platforms: The ecosystem.

The phone is just a piece of glass; the interface is the soul of the product.

03. Evaluation vs. Usage

10 MINS

Becoming a "Digital Critic"

Using is passive consumption (low brain activity). Evaluating is active analysis (high brain activity). We teach students to ask "Why" instead of "What."

Key Message: "Usage is about how you feel. Evaluation is about how the user performs a task."

Example: Comparing the checkout process of Amazon vs. a local small business site. Where does the user get lost?

04. The Financial Impact

15 MINS

Lifecycle & Economics

Evaluation isn't just a "final check." It is a continuous loop. We explain that finding a bug before launch costs $100, but finding it after launch costs $10,000 in lost customers.

The Evaluation Loop:

1. Formative (During Design)
2. Summative (Before Launch)
3. Continuous (After Launch)

Final Guided Discussion

Shift from User to Evaluator. What is one website you will never use again because of its poor interface?
